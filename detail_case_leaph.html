Case Study #2 : Leaph Street
Designing a new legal cannabis delivery app in NYC
Stakeholder: leaph street
Site: http:leaphstreet
Duration: September 2022 - April 2023
PROJECT OVERVIEW

Leaph Street makes it easier for people in NYC to find cannabis-based options throughout the city. With reports of.... Curently, people use a variery of sources to find local options. It takes a search across Google, Craigslist, Instagram & apps like Weed Maps. It's a time consuming process to find relevant and up to date infomation about what's available (and what's actually good!). As someone who has been canaabis based for almost 20 years, Im highly motivated to put in the work to find cannabis options, but for those starting to dabble, how can we expect them to do the same? What do I mean by "cannabis-based"? Cannabis based are ones derived from cannabis. Alternatively, "canabis" could be used to refer to an entire lifestyle. Unfortunately, cannabis-ism still carries a stigma that turns some people off.s The goal is to design and develop a legal cannabis delivery app that provides a seamless and secure user experience for customers in need of legal cannabis products. The app will allow customers to browse, purchase, and receive legal cannabis products directly to their door, while complying with all state and local regulations.

PROJECT GOALS

I hatched the idea for Leaph Street after trying to find a place to go for cannabis friendly options in our neighborhood. After much Googling, searching Craigslist ads and Instagram, I found one that wasn't sketchy. But it wasn't easy.

The goals of Leaph Street are to:

Be the source of premium cannabis based options and cannabis friendly locations in every NYC neighborhood:
Information is spread across many different sources, making it a time-consuming process to find relevant, curated and up to date information.


Appeal to anyone wanting cannabis based products:
According to one, .... are using THC or CBD products. There is a great opportunity to cater to them in an inclusive, non-judmental way.


Create a user friendly interface that allows far easy navigation:
To provide a secure and reliable platform for customers to make transactions and receive deliveries.


Overall we want to develop a brand identity that accurately represent the values and goals of Leaph Street.

PROJECT RISK

By nature, every cannabis adjacent business is young -- to be successful operating in this space, we're navigating multiple challenges: keeping abreast of the needs of a rapidly growing business, copycats & competitors, and keeping on top of the complications of compliance.


Legal compliance: ensure the app is compliant with local and state laws and regulations.


User adoption & Technical Issues: ensure the app is user-friendly and appeals to target audience and ensure the app functions properly and is reliable.


Competitors: There are quite a few cannabis finding apps out there-- the most well known being Weed Maps.


Leaph Street is a strategic, long term thinking company prepared to stay ahead of time or adjust our direction accordingly.

PROJECT CONCLUSION

In conclusion, the legalization of cannabis in New York City has led to a significant increase in its use. However, many people are still unsure about its use and there is a need for a centralized location where people can access accurate and up-to-date information. Our project aims to address this need by developing a platform that provides information about the legal and safe use of cannabis, as well as resources for those who may be struggling with addiction. By promoting safe and responsible use of cannabis, we hope to reduce the negative impacts of its use on the community and help people make informed decisions about their cannabis use. The successful completion of this project will result in a legal cannabis delivery app that provides a seamless and secure user experience for customers in need of legal cannabis products. The app will help ensure that customers have access to legal and regulated products, while providing a safe and convenient platform for delivery.

OPPORTUNITY
STATEMENT

The legalization of cannabis in New York State has created a significant opportunity for businesses to enter the market and capitalize on the growing demand for cannabis products. With the right strategy and execution, a cannabis company can establish itself as a leader in the industry and achieve substanstial growth and profitability. This opportunity presents itself in various forms, including:


Retail Dispensaries: Retail dispensaries are the primary distribution channels for legal cannabis products. A well-located dispensary with a wide selection of products and excellent customer service can attract a loyal customer base and generate significant revenue.


Cultivation and Production: As demand for cannabis products grows, so does the need for high-quality cannabis cultivation and production facilities. A legal cannabis company that can produce premium products at scale can establish itself as a leading supplier in the industry.


Technology and Innovation: The cannabis industry is relatively new and rapidly evolving. There is a significant opportunity for companies to develop innovative technologies to improve cultivation, production, and distribution processes.


Leaph Street will find itself useful in all aspects of the cannabis industry.

THEORY

As the demand for cannabis increases, so will the need for... First time and long time cannabis users will have better experience when they can connect with other people in their neighborhood to freely discuss and review cannabis co-ops.

DISCOVERY &
INSPIRATION

I've used the internet to search for cannabis more frequently since the legalization of cannabis in New York in 2021. After trying a few okay places I found online, I begin to want a more fine tuned experience based on my "needs". After many ... and ... I found myself a makeshift solution...I manually searched for businesses and created a google spread sheet of their product and quality. Fuck! I thought. There should be a better way to find quality products!


So I began designing one...

RESEARCH &
IDEATION

Curious about the need for a more integrated solution, I started exploring these questions through analysis and user interviews. Is this actually a problem worth solving?


COMPETITIVE ANALYSIS
Looking at some of the most popular cannabis apps currently available, I found that they tend to be for broad audiences and fairly general in nature. At the time, none of the products offered the community aspect.   Additionally, most of the apps focused on locations.  There weren't any solutions that focused on community, mood and --- of cannabis users in NYC.Leaph Street aims to change that by providing a central location for the best products in New York City curated by real cannabis users.​


USER INTERVIEWS
I created a set of questions to ask potential users. I was especially interested in responses around the following themes:

  1. What type of products are you typically interested in purchasing through an app?
  2. Have you had any positive or negative experiences with other apps/services in the past?
  3. Have you ever had any issues with the quality or accuracy of the products you've received through a delivery service?
  4. Would you be interested in a loyalty program or other incentives for using a app or product?
  5. How experienced is the user with cannabis, technology and community?
CREATING
PERSONAS

From the interview responses, I drafted my product idea and Minimum Viable Audience (MVA), which I used to create 4 user personas, whom I've named Ronnie, Bobby, Ricky, and Mike. Personas represent archetypes of real people who may use the product and services of Leaph Street. They help to understand and empathize with users as human beings with unique needs and goals, rather than just numbers or statistics.

RONNIE DEVOE Limited experience with cannabis products and rely heavily on the expertise of budtenders to recommend appropriate strains and dosages. They value safety and consistency and prioritize products with clear labeling and consistent dosages. medical marijuana patient, 61
BOBBY BROWN They are interested in exploring new strains and value novelty and variety. They are price-sensitive and prefer affordable options. They prioritize products that offer a high-quality experience, including pleasant flavor and aroma. Enjoys using cannabis for its social uses.. recreational user, 25
RICKY BELL An experienced user interested in rare strains and enjoy exploring unique and artisanal products. They prioritize products that offer a high level of quality and consistency and are willing to pay a premium for exceptional products. Has extensive knowledge of cannabis products. experienced user, 40
MIKE BIVENS Interested in exploring products that offer specific health benefits, such as reducing anxiety or improving sleep and prioritize products that are organic, non-GMO, and free from harmful chemicals. They are willing to pay a premium for high-quality, health-focused products. health & wellness enthusiast, 52

THE
EVOLUTION

Previous to Leaph Street, I’d only ever built pretty basic websites with HTML/CSS and minimal Javascript like this website, so it was a pretty steep learning curve to work with something like React Native, as well as APIs and a database. Design+Code’s React Native for Designers was a really helpful starting point to build off of.


Once I got the front-end (the UI and navigation) in place with static data, next came the daunting task of setting up the backend with Firestore, Google’s NoSQL database, to store, sync and query actual data. I hadn’t a clue how this really worked, but piece by piece, tutorial by tutorial, I got things working.

Eventually I stopped designing in Sketch and just created new screens directly in code using components I’d already built.

ROLE &
PROCESS

After researching and designing the first iteration of Leaph Street, I then faced the daunting task of actually building an app. I took this as an opportunity to stack my front end coding skills and learn React Native. I’ve learned a TON about mobile development in this time, including integrating Firebase, Cloud Functions, Algolia, SendGrid, and more. I’m excited to bring the knowledge and skills to future teams that I work with!
